# Kings & Queens Art Guide — Ragnarok Card Game
## AI Art Generation Reference (13 Kings & Queens)

Each entry includes: **Flavor Text**, **Visual Art Prompt**, and **Key Iconic Attributes**.

Kings in this game serve as passive commanders — they sit behind the battlefield, radiating auras that shape the entire game. Their art should feel **monumental, ancient, and immovable** — like forces of nature rather than fighters. They don't hold weapons; they ARE the power.

Queens and playable King heroes are full combat participants with hero powers and weapons. Their art should feel **regal, dangerous, and actively engaged**.

# PRIMORDIAL KINGS (9)
*These are the cosmic forces that existed before the gods. Each one is a battlefield commander — not a fighter, but the gravitational center around which everything orbits. Their art should feel like creation myths given form.*

## 1. Ymir — The Primordial Jotunn
**Role:** Tempo Pressure / Attack Skew | **Gender:** Male

**Flavor Text:**
> "Before there were gods, before there were worlds, the collision of fire and ice in Ginnungagap birthed two brothers: Ymir and Brimir. Ymir became flesh and frost — solid, immovable, the body from which worlds would be carved. His brother became blood and fury. Together they were the first breath of creation, twin forces of the same primordial instant. From Ymir's flesh the earth was shaped, from his bones the mountains, from his skull the sky. He does not fight — he simply exists, and existence bends around him."

**AI Art Prompt:**
Colossal primordial frost giant, impossibly ancient and vast, sitting cross-legged on a throne carved from a glacier the size of a mountain. His body is a fusion of living flesh and ancient ice — blue-white skin cracked like tundra permafrost, with veins of glowing glacial blue running beneath. His face is broad, weathered, and impassive like a cliff face — deep-set eyes that glow like frozen stars, a massive jaw covered in a beard of actual icicles that hang to his chest. His torso is bare and enormous, muscles like tectonic plates beneath frost-rimed skin. Jagged ice formations grow from his shoulders and spine like crystalline wings. His hands rest on his knees, palms upward, with rivers of meltwater flowing from his fingers. Behind him: the primordial void — a swirling collision of fire (Muspelheim, orange-red) and ice (Niflheim, pale blue) meeting in cosmic chaos. Tiny silhouettes of mountains, rivers, and oceans emerge from his body — the world being born from his form. Scale: he should dwarf everything. Color palette: glacial blue, deep frost-white, ancient grey stone, veins of molten amber where fire meets ice. Epic cosmic fantasy, sense of geological time and impossible scale.

**Key Visual Attributes:**
- Colossal scale (mountains are small beside him)
- Body fused with ice — cracked permafrost skin, glacial veins
- Icicle beard hanging to chest
- Fire-and-ice collision behind him (Muspelheim meets Niflheim)
- Rivers flowing from his fingertips
- Mountains and landforms emerging from his body
- Seated on a glacier throne — immovable, eternal

## 2. Buri — The First God
**Role:** Attrition / Scaling Defense | **Gender:** Male

**Flavor Text:**
> "Licked free from the salt-ice by Auðumbla over three endless days, Buri emerged not as a helpless newborn but as perfection itself — the template from which all divinity would descend. He speaks no words. He carries no weapon. He simply endures, and in enduring, he becomes eternal. Patience is his dominion, and time is his only ally."

**AI Art Prompt:**
The first god emerging from a massive wall of primordial salt-ice, mid-liberation — his upper body free, radiant and perfect, while his lower half remains encased in translucent glacial ice that glows from within. His form is idealized masculine beauty in the Norse tradition — tall, broad-shouldered, with a strong jaw and serene, almost meditative expression. His skin has a faint golden luminance, as if light itself chose to become flesh. Long silver-white hair flows upward as if suspended in water, defying gravity. No armor, no weapons — his bare chest bears intricate frost-patterns that resemble Norse knotwork, formed naturally in his skin. His eyes are closed or half-lidded, radiating calm golden light from behind the lids. One arm is raised, fingers spread, and a translucent golden shield-dome radiates outward from his palm — a perfect hemisphere of protective light. The ice around him shows the tongue-marks of the great cosmic cow, long gouges revealing the god beneath. Behind: an infinite white expanse — the primordial nothing before creation, with faint aurora borealis ribbons forming in response to his emergence. Color palette: warm gold luminance, pristine ice-white, silver hair, faint aurora greens and violets. Ethereal, divine, serene. Classical beauty meets Norse mythology.

**Key Visual Attributes:**
- Emerging from salt-ice wall (half-encased)
- Idealized divine form — golden-glowing skin
- Silver-white hair floating upward
- Eyes closed in serene meditation
- Frost knotwork patterns on skin (natural, not tattoos)
- Golden shield-dome radiating from palm
- Tongue-gouges visible in the ice (Auðumbla's marks)
- No weapons, no armor — pure divine essence

Cosmic aurora winds through the branches. Color palette: deep bark brown, living emerald green, golden leaves, nine-world color spectrum, cosmic violet background. Awe-inspiring, cosmic, alive.

## 8. Brimir — The Bloody Moisture, Brother of Ymir
**Role:** Aggression Through Death | **Gender:** Male

**Flavor Text:**
> "Born alongside Ymir from the same collision of fire and ice in the great void, Brimir was the other half of creation's first breath. Where Ymir became flesh and frost — solid, immovable, the body from which worlds would be carved — Brimir became blood and marrow, the violent current that gave those worlds their color. Brothers forged from the same primordial instant, yet opposite in nature: Ymir the mountain, Brimir the eruption. Some scholars whisper that the Eddas obscured their kinship, but the truth was always in the blood. Every death in Brimir's presence is not an ending but a detonation — the same blood that once flowed through creation's first-born twins now spills outward, and what it touches, it destroys."

**AI Art Prompt:**
Primordial entity of blood and bone, brother of Ymir, standing in a landscape that is literally being created from carnage. His body is humanoid but wrong — his skin is translucent and red, like looking through a membrane of blood, and beneath it you can see rivers of crimson flowing instead of veins, and bones that shift and rearrange like tectonic plates. His face shares a faint familial resemblance to Ymir — the same impossibly broad jaw and deep-set eyes — but where Ymir is cold and glacial, Brimir is hot and raw. His face is gaunt and sharp, with hollow cheeks and a wide mouth that splits too far, revealing teeth like broken bone fragments. His eyes are solid red — no iris, no pupil, just deep arterial crimson that weeps constant thin streams of blood down his cheeks like war-paint tears. He wears no clothing — his body IS his armor, the surface of his skin hardening into bone-plate in some areas and remaining raw and wet in others. From his back, jagged formations of bone extend upward like a grotesque crown or spine-crest, dripping with perpetual blood. A faint blue-white frost pattern on his left shoulder is the only visible remnant of his shared origin with Ymir — frost meeting blood where the brothers once were one. The ground beneath him is a churning mixture of blood and primordial clay, and from this red mud, shapes are forming — mountains, rivers, creatures — the world being born from violence. Behind him: a blood-red sky with dark clouds, and the massive silhouette of Ymir's glacier-throne visible in the far distance — brother watching brother across the void. Color palette: arterial crimson, bone-white, raw flesh pink, dark blood-brown, storm-grey sky, with a subtle glacial blue accent. Visceral, mythic, beautiful in its horror — creation through destruction.

**Key Visual Attributes:**
- Translucent red skin showing blood-rivers beneath
- Faint familial resemblance to Ymir (broad jaw, deep-set eyes)
- Frost pattern on left shoulder (shared origin with brother Ymir)
- Shifting bone-plate formations on body
- Solid crimson eyes weeping blood-tears
- Bone-crest crown/spine extending from back
- Ground churning with blood-clay birthing landforms
- Ymir's glacier-throne silhouette visible in the far distance
- No clothing — body itself is armor
- Teeth like broken bone fragments
- Perpetually dripping, wet, visceral

## 9. Ginnungagap — Primordial Chaos
**Role:** Chaos / Variance / Adaptation | **Gender:** None (Abstract Entity)

**Flavor Text:**
> "Before fire, before ice, before the first thought — there was the Yawning Void. Not empty. Not full. Simply undefined. Ginnungagap is the possibility that existed before possibility had a name. In its presence, anything can happen, and nothing is certain. Every moment is a dice roll. Every breath is creation or destruction, chosen at random."

**AI Art Prompt:**
An abstract cosmic entity representing the primordial void itself — not a person but a PRESENCE. The central form is a humanoid-shaped absence — a silhouette cut from reality, where the outline of a massive seated figure contains swirling, formless chaos within. Inside the silhouette: galaxies forming and collapsing, fire and ice colliding and separating, half-formed creatures flickering in and out of existence, geometric shapes dissolving into fractals. The edges of the silhouette crackle and shift — sometimes sharp, sometimes dissolving into static, like a television tuned between channels. The figure's "head" area contains two asymmetric vortexes where eyes would be — one spiraling inward (destruction/consumption) and one spiraling outward (creation/birth), both in different colors (one deep purple, one molten gold). Around the entity: small void-spawn creatures materialize from the chaos — each one different, some with wings, some with multiple legs, some geometric, all translucent and flickering like holograms struggling to exist. The background is the Ginnungagap itself — not black, not white, but an impossible color that shifts between them, with distant pinpricks of the first light trying to break through. Streams of raw potential (visualized as liquid aurora borealis) flow from the entity's hands into the void, where they randomly crystallize into miniature trees, stones, flames, ice crystals, and creatures before dissolving again. Color palette: impossible void-color (shifting black/white/grey), deep cosmic purple, molten creation-gold, aurora greens and magentas, static-grey. Mind-bending, abstract, the visual equivalent of staring into infinity.

**Key Visual Attributes:**
- Humanoid-shaped ABSENCE (silhouette cut from reality)
- Chaos swirling inside the silhouette (galaxies, fire, ice, half-formed life)
- Two asymmetric vortex-eyes (one inward/purple, one outward/gold)
- Edges crackling and shifting like static
- Void-spawn creatures materializing around it (each unique)
- Liquid aurora flowing from hands, crystallizing randomly
- Ginnungagap backdrop — impossible shifting void-color
- Not a being but a CONCEPT given barely-visible form

# QUEEN CHESS PIECES (24 Heroes)

*In Ragnarok Chess, the Queen is the most powerful piece on the board — the primary spellcaster with 10 spell slots and access to Mage, Warlock, and Necromancer spell pools. # STYLE NOTES FOR ALL KINGS & QUEENS

**Consistent Art Direction:**

- **Resolution**: All portraits should be high-detail digital art, 1:1 square ratio (card portrait format)
- **Framing**: Chest-up to full-body, centered composition, character dominant
- **Style**: Epic fantasy digital painting — detailed, painterly, dramatic lighting
- **Background**: Each character's domain/realm should be visible but not competing with the figure
- **Mood**: Monumental for Primordial Kings, Arcane-Powerful for Queen chess pieces
- **Negative prompts**: No anime style, no cartoon, no chibi, no modern clothing, no photorealistic, no AI artifacts (extra fingers, melted faces)

**Primordial Kings should feel like:**

- Forces of nature, not warriors
- Ancient beyond comprehension
- Seated or standing still — power at rest
- Their environment responds to them (ground melts, ice forms, plants grow)

**Queen Chess Pieces should feel like:**

- The most powerful piece on the board — masters of arcane force
- Wielding magic, time, death, or cosmic power (not physical weapons)
- Both male and female heroes can hold the Queen role
- Their spellcasting nature should be visually evident (glowing hands, magical artifacts, arcane energy)
- Dangerous intelligence — these are the strategists, not the brutes

**Cross-Mythology Variants should feel like:**

- Culturally authentic to their mythology (Japanese, Egyptian)
- Distinct visual language from Norse/Greek heroes
- Same power fantasy, different aesthetic tradition
